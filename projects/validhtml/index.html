David D. Diamond

Introduction

Dave Diamond is a retired Unrestricted Naval Aviator who served for over 20 years in the U.S. Navy and Naval Reserve. He holds a commercial pilot license for fixed and rotary wing aircraft and flew over 3,600 hours with over 2,000 of those hours flown as a helicopter flight instructor and check ride pilot.

Dave went to Tulane University on a Navy ROTC scholarship. He later earned his Master's Degree at the University of West Florida in Industrial/Organizational Psychology. For his thesis he designed and performed a NASA funded human factors experiment that was published in a British journal.

After leaving the Navy, he worked in the defense industry as a Personnel Psychologist, military and aviation Subject Matter Expert (SME), Research Psychologist, Human Performance Engineer, and Instructional Systems Designer.

He has developed training systems for HMX-1, the Royal Saudi Air Force, the Singapore Air Force, the Royal Navy, and numerous U.S. Defense Support agencies.

He is currently attending Seminole State College in the Orlando, Florida area to learn new skills in web development and photography.
